  <h1>How To Make Tonkatsu</h1>
  <p>Tonkatsu is a type of fried pork cutlet from Japan that is coated with egg and panko.  This savory food pairs well with some plain white rice and curry.</p>
  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>2 boneless pork chops</li>
    <li>2 eggs(yolks and whites)</li>
    <li>panko</li>
    <li>salt and pepper(to taste)</li>
    <li>oil</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Instructions</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Put oil in a pan and heat the pan to medium-high heat.</li>
    <li>Beat eggs until both the whites and yolks are mixed and pat dry pork chops.</li>
    <li>Put panko in seperate container that is big enough to put an entire pork chop inside of and add salt and pepper to panko</li>
    <li>Dip each pork chop in the egg mixture, making sure to flip it so both sides have the egg mixture on it.</li>
    <li>Dip each egg coated pork chop into the panko, making sure each side is completely coated in the mixture.</li>
    <li>Put the pork chops in the heated pan, frying until golden brown, then flipping to fry the other side.</li>
    <li>Once completely fried, put on a plate and let rest for five minutes.  Then you can severe with your choice of sides.</li>
  </ol>
